Maven partnership

Discovery confirms strong commercial outcomes — Phase 1 targets manual handling & fragmented tooling

We’ll keep Mercury and PowerApps stable, cut repeated documentation work, and prepare for AI-driven workflow design.

Discovery highlights

85–95 hrs/week spent on documentation, market mapping, and content prep across the cohort; duplicated entry and offline notes slow recruiters.

Mercury + PowerApps deliver the backbone, but fragmentation and manual conversions limit scale, flexibility, and future AI workflows.

Phase 1 focus
  • Reduce repeated write-ups and simplify routine logging.
  • Structured data capture with one source of truth (Dataverse).
  • Stabilize Mercury/PowerApps while setting up future automation & AI.
Constraints acknowledged

Protect current productivity, avoid disruption to active searches, and align with security/role permissions.

Continuity first Governance Adoption
Core activity buckets

Where time concentrates

  • Market Mapping: adding/updating contacts, researching moves, staying aware of updates.
  • Mandate Activities: initial longlists, ensuring mandate contacts are up to date, consolidating shortlists.
  • Knowledge Share: capturing notes from calls/emails, summarising, searching notes/Teams for insights.
Findings

Fragmentation & visibility gaps

  • Parallel Excel trackers create multiple sources of truth and extra updates.
  • Repeated conversion from raw notes to structured fields slows logging.
  • Knowledge is hard to surface/search across Teams, Viva, contact notes.
  • Leadership visibility limited to static Power BI exports; little drill-through.
Foundational work
Data consolidation
Migrate market mapping & mandate data from Excel/Shadow IT into Dataverse (4–6 weeks team-by-team).
Roles & access
Configure roles, permissions, and authentication to support new experiences (~2 weeks).
Front-end setup
Establish scalable interface patterns and documentation (~1 week).
Functional solutions
Streamline key activities
  • Structured capture and note consolidation to cut duplicate write-ups.
  • Faster logging pathways between early capture and system entry.
  • One-source-of-truth pipelines for mandates and market mapping.
Enable new capabilities
  • Self-service insights for leaders (drill-through beyond static exports).
  • Searchable knowledge surfaces across notes, Teams, and market intel.
  • AI-ready workflow design once data is consolidated and governed.
